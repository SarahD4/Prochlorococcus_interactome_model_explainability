Reading ppiGPLM with the Jacobian Lens: where does "this pair interacts" actually live?

A representation-level complement to the manuscript's DeepLift / Integrated-Gradients / alanine-ablation N-terminal paradox. jacobian-lens was fit on ppiGPLM's own residual stream (719 real MED4 pairs, layers 0–10, RTX 4080) and applied to ask: at each layer, does a position's hidden state — transported into the model's final decision basis — already look like “1” (interacts) rather than “0”?

Causal test: activation patching confirms the effect is concentrated at the peak positions

Everything above is correlational (does a position's representation look like the decision). This test is causal: the raw residual stream at layer 9 — not the lens-transported version, since that's what the rest of the network actually consumes — is patched at specific positions from a donor prompt into a recipient prompt's own forward pass, and the resulting shift in logit("1")−logit("0") at the final position is measured against the recipient's unpatched baseline. 150 index-matched real/random pairs; peak = positions 6–8 (protein1) or 6–8 (protein2); control = positions 0–1 (protein1) or position 1 only (protein2, since position 0 was already shown to carry a weak signal from cross-attention onto protein1).

In 3 of 4 protein×direction combinations, patching the peak positions moves the decision significantly more than patching the null-signal control positions — the causal effect really is concentrated where the representational read-out said it would be. The exception is protein1's random→real direction, where neither peak nor control patches meaningfully disturb an already-confident "interacts" call. Absolute shifts are small relative to the multi-unit logit margins that separate confident real/random predictions: a 3-residue, single-layer patch is a narrow intervention against a decision the model builds from the whole sequence, so the finding is about where the causal leverage concentrates, not that these positions alone determine the outcome.

What this adds to the correlation–causation–context framework

Three independent methodologies now dissociate at the N-terminus, not two:
  • Attribution (DeepLift/IG): N-terminal residues, especially position 0, carry the largest causal effect on the output logit.
  • Structure (AlphaFold3): N-terminal residues are depleted at the physical binding interface.
  • Representation (this analysis): position 0–1's own hidden state does not linearly decode as "interacts" in the model's own final-layer basis — that signal is strongest a few residues downstream (~4–8) and downstream again toward the C-terminus.

That pattern — high causal leverage without the position itself carrying the "yes" concept — is the behavioral signature of a gate or modulator, not a direct feature detector: position 0–1 appears to condition how later positions' representations get formed (via attention), rather than encoding the interaction verdict itself. This sharpens rather than resolves the paradox, and is most consistent with the manuscript's allosteric communication and specificity coding hypotheses over pure structural scaffolding — and offers a concrete, narrower follow-up target (positions ~4–8, not the extreme N-terminus) for mutagenesis or motif-scanning follow-up.

Running the same readout symmetrically on protein2 confirms this is a general property of the model's decision-making, not a protein1-specific quirk: the same N-term/middle/C-term ranking, the same near-null first-two-residues region, and slightly larger effect sizes throughout. The one clean asymmetry — protein2's position 0 carries a small but real signal that protein1's does not — is exactly what the causal (autoregressive) architecture predicts: protein2's first residue is already conditioned on the entirety of protein1, while protein1's first residue has no interaction context at all yet. That asymmetry is itself a useful confirmation that the lens is tracking something architecturally real, not noise.

The activation-patching test above closes the loop: it is not just that peak positions read as more interaction-like than control positions, but that overwriting them causally moves the model's decision significantly more than overwriting the null-signal control positions, in 3 of 4 tested directions. Reading and intervention agree.

Model: ppiGPLM (nanoGPT-style, 12L/12H/768d, 84.98M params, char-level 29-token vocab), checkpoint out_3e/ckpt.pt from GreenGenomicsLab/Prochlorococcus_interactome_model_explainability (HF). Lens: anthropics/jacobian-lens, fit with skip_first=6 (overriding the library default of 16, which would otherwise discard the N-terminus as an "attention sink" position) on 719 PRS prompts, target layer 11, dim_batch=16, converged to relative change <5×10−4. Protein2 readouts use the same fitted lens (no refitting), with position offsets computed from the "<ps1>," / ",<ps2>," marker lengths. All p-values two-sided Mann–Whitney U except the activation-patching test, which uses the two-sided Wilcoxon signed-rank test (paired across donor–recipient pairs).
